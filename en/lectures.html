    After studying all the details of a subject, a dedicated person becomes an expert. In other words, if you read and study 50 books on a subject, you will know almost everything that exists about it. But reading is not enough, it is necessary to apply and test.

    I have given lectures at schools and universities.

    When I present a lecture, I make it in such a way that not a single minute is wasted. I hate speakers that make people look like idiots asking them to jump, to dance, to massage others, to scream like bears and abuse storytelling to teach very little in the end. In the same way that some movies with bad stories fill scenes with a lot of special effects and lewdness to hold attention, some "speakers" without content do weird things to pass the time. In one hour it is possible to teach many things if the time is well used by the speaker. There are a lot of people giving lectures to say obvious things. Expect something special, detailed and scientific from me. I don't like to delay a second to start and I always value all the people who watch.

    On my Youtube channel there are 4 lectures (in Portuguese and in English) that I presented before. In the future I will give lectures on other topics and post. When I set out to make a lecture I only have one mindset, do the best available on the subject or nothing at all. I don't get involved to do something if it's not to do the best.

    Some of the topics that I feel prepared to present are:

    • - how to learn languages
    • - how memory works
    • - how to study efficiently
    • - how to manage time
    • - coaching (using different perspectives)
    • - how languages influence thinking
    • - how languages interfere in the learning of mathematics
    • - cultural differences between countries
    • - how to change habits
    • - motivation (how it works and how to maintain it)
    • - high performance in life for athletes, executives, students etc
    • - neuroscience of learning

    I really enjoy giving lectures. I learned public speaking by watching a few good speakers and reading books on the subject.

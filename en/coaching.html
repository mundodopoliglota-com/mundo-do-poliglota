    Before getting to the main point, I need to explain something. It is impossible to understand what coaching is without knowing the origin of this word.


    Around the year 1400, in a small Hungarian town called Kocs, some wheel maker began to produce a type of carriage with a steel spring suspension. The name of this carriage was “kocsi szekér”. The word “szekér” means “chariot” in Hungarian. And “kocsi” is a reference to the city, as it is formed by “Kocs” (the name of the city) and “i” (Hungarian suffix to refer to the origin). A translation of "kocsi szekér" would be "Kocsian carriage".

    From Hungarian, this word entered the languages of countries bordering Hungary. In Czech one says “kočár”, in Slovak “koč” and eventually it reached German in the form of “Kutsche” and Dutch as “koets”. In Spanish and Portuguese, it arrived as “coche”. In Brazil, the term “coche” to refer to a carriage is not common, but it remained within the word “cocheiro” (person who drives the carriage), for example. And finally, in English, the term that arrived is “coach”.

    I once saw a famous Brazilian coach saying that the word “coach” came from “kocsi”, and that “kocsi” was a carriage in Hungary. As I study Hungarian and speak to some extent, I realized that this explanation is wrong. He may have read this information from a book by someone else who also didn't know and started to transmit it wrongly in his courses. By the way, I doubt that most people who work with some kind of coaching know this, because it's a detail that only those who know Hungarian would notice! Currently “kocsi” means “car” in Hungarian.

    I saw a course booklet by this same guy and noticed that it had errors in the parts where he tries to use neuroscientific terms. Every time someone tries to use a scientific term or a word from another language to impress, even if the person catches the attention of many and gets very rich selling expensive courses, he will always be perceived as he really is by those who know the words and the science.

    Not long ago, a major TV channel made a program talking about the existence of charlatanism in coaching in Brazil. The biggest course sellers made videos advocating coaching and they are right to do so. But if they took the time to reflect, they would realize that they had created this problem themselves. When someone sells a course to thousands of people and distributes certificates without evaluating the quality of the participants, it causes that amount of people, which includes people who are unprepared, illiterate, rogue etc., to do things as they want and without quality. The same problem exists in other professions. It is necessary to carefully select someone who will work with people. Not everyone has the talent to deal with very different people. Coaching courses should be stricter about who can take them.

    In the United States, this term was initially connected to sports. In the 90's, Anthony Robbins started to use it for advice on life issues and called it "life coaching". From it, others started to copy the word and use it for several other subjects that already existed before, but with it they were better translated. I've read his books, been to his event in the US, and I understand why this guy is so idolized by coaching audiences around the world. But, I'm against idolatry and my iconoclastic part sees some things I don't agree with in his approaches. Just to give you an idea, in the event I was, Anthony Robbins asked a woman “When was the last time you gave your husband a blowjob?” and there was too much aggression for him to sell his upcoming seminars with secrets to success. In one of the approaches, an employee of his said that anyone who did not have 10 thousand dollars to register for his next seminar, could make a loan. I don't like that sort of thing. People should be respected and there are things some people do that go beyond the line. Some people who idolize him turn a blind eye to these practices.

That is, the person wants more money, but doesn't care about becoming more intelligent, or in another case, the person wants to have a relationship, but doesn't realize that they drive people away with their attitudes.

    The point is that everyone needs help. Usually people do not know how they are perceived by others and judge others based on feelings.

    There is a huge advantage for anyone in getting help. Help can be advice from a friend, advice from an expert, a consultation with an intelligent doctor etc. We all need help and advice at different times in life.

    In the world of science and philosophy, relationships where someone received guidance from another who already knew the way had always existed. Aristotle, perhaps one of the greatest philosophers of all time, was a student of Plato for 20 years. Alexander III of Macedonia (known as Alexander the Great) was responsible for conquering the Persian Empire, Syria and Egypt in just 12 years. What many do not know is that he was a student of Aristotle since he was a child. Think of the size of the advantage he had in being a student of Aristotle. Aristotle saw several cities failing and others thriving and identified all the elements that lead to the prosperity of a people and an individual. He was an intellectual who covered almost all areas of knowledge in his time and left a vast literary production relevant to this day.

    Physicist Isaac Newton must have been one of the most intelligent people to ever walk the Earth. He once wrote in a letter the phrase "If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of Giants." With this phrase, Newton referred to the great scientists who existed before him, such as Kepler, Galileo etc. In 2017, physicist Stephen Hawking said: "Each generation stands on the shoulders of those that came before, just as I did when I was a doctoral student at Cambridge, inspired by the work of Isaac Newton, James Clerk Maxwell and Albert Einstein."

    Michael Faraday is a less famous physicist, but he had a spectacular trajectory. Ever since he was a child he wanted to be a scientist, but he didn't know how. Eventually he met a chemist named Humphry Davy and became his assistant in his laboratory. The years passed and he learned in practice how to be a scientist and surpassed his master. He performed several original experiments and left his name in the history of science.

    If it's not obvious to you yet, I repeat, everyone needs help! These great philosophers, scientists and emperors of the past recognized that they didn't know everything and needed people to guide them. If the great do not hesitate to seek help, it is clear that seeking help is an act of greatness. Therefore, we must not be arrogant and think that we have the solutions for everything. It is often better to check how someone has already solved a problem you are experiencing than to reinvent the wheel, because not all people in the world are able to create new wheels. Anyone who wants to improve needs knowledge. Only people who want to grow see themselves for who they really are and do not ignore their own limitations. A different perspective from someone with knowledge can make a huge difference in a person's life.

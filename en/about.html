    My name is Higor Pereira. I am a neuroscientist, polyglot and language teacher. For over a decade I have been obsessed with discovering the best strategies, techniques and tools for learning languages. Many people think that I became a polyglot because of neuroscience, but it is the opposite. I could already speak 6 languages before starting my neuroscience degree.

    I dropped out of another course (aerospace engineering) when I decided to study neuroscience. The university degree in neuroscience brought me explanations and names for many approaches that I already knew. Before neuroscience I felt that I was missing something to understand what I did out of passion, vocation and mission.

    In addition to allowing me to understand in-depth all the details about the language learning process, I also gained the ability to understand the world from a scientific perspective. Besides psycholinguistics (part of neuroscience that studies language acquisition) and neurolinguistics (part of neuroscience that studies the neural mechanisms involved in language), I continue to study topics in other very interesting areas of neuroscience such as: cognition (intelligence, IQ, memory, creativity etc.), neuroeconomics (decision making and neuromarketing) and neuropsychopharmacology.

    I am also an enthusiast of coaching, because I see many possibilities to improve the life of a person or a business using neuroscientific concepts. In my free time I usually read books on different topics (biographies, mathematics, history, leadership, management, world literature and philosophy).

    I currently speak 6 languages fluently (Portuguese, English, Spanish, German, Swedish and French), I speak 2 at an intermediate level (Dutch and Italian) and 1 at a basic level (Hungarian). As soon as I reach a higher level in Hungarian, I will study more languages.

    In addition to languages, neuroscience and coaching, I am also interested in everything that facilitates learning. I know that the traditional approach to language teaching is wrong and I think the same happens for other subjects. That is, the teaching of mathematics, history, literature etc. can be improved in different ways.

    I am vegetarian, I practice calisthenics and I like to jump on the trampoline.

    I have been to 23 countries so far and I am going to visit many more.
